September Memories & Plans

2017.09.10

September is associated in my mind with crafting - specifically, with sewing. For a period in my childhood, my mother would sew Halloween costumes for my sister and me, so early fall would find us visiting the fabric store, where we would look through pattern books and plan our costumes. Most of all, I remember the palette of colors that surrounded me on those trips: earthy reds and browns and vivid oranges, flowing through the aisles of fabric bolts and winking from the pages of the pattern books. In spite of the fluorescent lighting and bland linoleum floors of the store, those colors made the place enchanting to me; they heightened my awareness of the many possible forms that could be shaped from the materials around me, and of the potential for those forms to reflect the larger world.

Crochet, not sewing, is my craft of choice at the moment (though I hope to have a sewing room sometime in the next few years). But no matter what crafting tools are at hand, the impulse to make something is there - and it is strengthened, somehow, by the change in season. No doubt that's partly because the prospect of cold weather makes it very easy to envision new crochet projects - but part of the reason, too, I think, must be that I was first introduced to the power and potential of craft at this time of year.

As for what I intend to make... Well, if there's one thing I learned from my experiences last year, my first year of crocheting, it's that the season very quickly gets ahead of my works in progress! So for this fall, I'm trying not to saddle myself with a huge backlog of WIPs. I've started a raglan cardigan, which needs sleeves and about twice its current length, and I'd also like to make a hat/scarf set out of some plum-colored yarn that I bought last year. By the time I get through all of that, it may well be December. I hope to record a few notes and photos as I go. We'll see!
